Reforestation is not a conservation amenity — it is water infrastructure. Restoring New Mexico's forests is essential to the long-term viability of our watersheds, our communities, and our climate resilience.
What We Do

Science-to-Landscape Reforestation

From the greenhouse bench to the hillside, NMRC connects research, production, and on-the-ground restoration into a coordinated statewide effort.

Applied Research

Generating the ecological and physiological data needed to make reforestation decisions at scale — from species selection and provenance matching to drought tolerance and post-planting survival.

View research areas

Seedling Production

Scaling native seedling production of regionally adapted conifers and hardwoods through partner nursery infrastructure — creating a reliable, research-backed supply chain for landscape-scale restoration.

Learn about production

Long-Term Monitoring

Tracking survival, growth, and ecosystem function across restoration sites to build the evidence base for adaptive management — and to demonstrate that reforestation investments yield lasting ecological returns.

About NMRC

A Coordinated Response to a Landscape-Scale Crisis

New Mexico's forests are under sustained pressure from drought, bark beetle, wildfire, and a warming climate. Millions of acres have experienced significant mortality in recent decades, with downstream consequences for watershed function, community water supplies, and carbon storage.

NMRC was established to provide the research infrastructure, production capacity, and institutional coordination that reforestation at this scale demands. Our multi-institutional structure brings together university researchers, state agencies, federal land managers, and tribal partners under a shared scientific framework.
