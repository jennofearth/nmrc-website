Partners & Team

Built on Institutional Collaboration

NMRC's reach depends on a network of research, management, and funding partners who share the goal of restoring New Mexico's forests at meaningful scale.

Institutional Partners

Our Partner Network

NMRC operates through a collaborative structure that spans university research programs, state and federal agencies, tribal governments, and nonprofit partners.

University & Research Institutions

NMSU

New Mexico State University

Administrative home and primary research institution for NMRC. Faculty in the College of Agricultural, Consumer and Environmental Sciences lead core research programs.

UNM

University of New Mexico

Collaborative research on forest ecology, disturbance dynamics, and watershed hydrology in upper Rio Grande systems.

NMHU

New Mexico Highlands University

Regional partner contributing applied research capacity and student training in forest restoration and natural resources management.

State Agencies

NMSF

NM State Forestry Division

Key operational partner for reforestation planning, post-fire recovery coordination, and nursery production on state-managed lands.

NMDGF

NM Dept. of Game & Fish

Collaboration on wildlife habitat restoration, post-fire reforestation in game-managed areas, and joint monitoring protocols.

EMNRD

Energy, Minerals & Natural Resources Dept.

Interagency coordination on forest health, watershed function, and alignment between reforestation priorities and state natural resource policy.

Federal & Nonprofit Partners

USFS

USDA Forest Service

Research collaboration with the Rocky Mountain Research Station and operational coordination with New Mexico's National Forests on restoration planting and monitoring.

NRCS

USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service

Federal funder and collaborator on watershed-scale restoration research and implementation programs serving private and tribal landowners.

RGWF

Rio Grande Water Fund

Strategic alignment with RGWF's large-scale forest health and watershed restoration investment in the upper Rio Grande basin.

Leadership

NMRC Leadership

NMRC is led by a small core team with deep expertise in forest ecology, restoration science, water resources, and institutional partnership management.

Work With Us

Partnership Opportunities

NMRC engages partners across several distinct pathways, depending on institutional type and interest.

Research Collaboration

We welcome co-investigator relationships with university and agency researchers working on complementary questions. NMRC can provide access to field sites, monitoring networks, seedling production infrastructure, and data sets.

Land Management Partners

Federal and state land managers can engage NMRC as a technical resource for reforestation planning, planting specification development, and monitoring program design on managed lands across New Mexico.

Funding & Grants

Foundations, utilities, and state water programs interested in supporting forest-watershed restoration research can discuss collaborative funding arrangements with NMRC leadership directly.
